Pronouns


Overview of Grammar topics:

  • A pronoun is a word that can replace a noun in a sentence.

  • Personal pronouns are pronouns that we use to refer to people and, sometimes, animals.

  • A subject pronoun takes the place of the subject of the sentence, which is the noun doing the action.

  • A possessive pronoun is a pronoun that expresses possession or ownership.

  • A demonstrative pronoun is a word used to stand in for a noun. They are used to point to something or someone specific.

  • A reflexive pronoun is a pronoun used as an object that refers to the same person or thing as the subject.

  • An interrogative pronoun is a pronoun used to ask a question directly or indirectly.
Type Usage Example
Personal pronouns refer to people and, sometimes, animals I, you, she, he, it, we, they, me, us, them
Possessive pronouns expresses possession or ownership mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs
Demonstrative pronouns used to point to specific things this, that, these, those
Reflexive pronouns used as an object of a verb that refers to the same person myself, yourself, itself, herself, himself, ourselves, themselves
Interrogative pronouns used to ask questions about unknown people or things who, whom, what, which, whose
